Boiled Eggs

Photo by Antoni Shkraba Studio

Description

If you have ever struggled getting your boiled eggs to be exactly the way you want them, give this recipe a try!

This recipe aims to prioritize eggs that are easy to peel and not overcooked.

Ingredients

  • Eggs, 12 large, uncooked
  • Water, tap
  • Ice Cubes, 12
  • Kosher Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, freshly cracked, to taste

Steps

  1. Remove eggs from fridge to begin tempering slightly
  2. Add 4 quarts (2 quarts per 6 eggs) of water to a large pot, cover, and bring to a rolling boil
  3. Once water is boiling, carefully add eggs to the pot a few at a time (however many you can safely add without splashing)
  4. Watch for water to return to rolling boil, then let continue for 30 seconds
  5. Add ice cubes to pot, then watch for water to return to a simmer
  6. Reduce heat to low and maintain water at a sub-simmer according to the below time-table:
    • Soft-Boiled: 5-6 minutes
    • Medium-Boiled: 7-8 minutes
    • Hard-Boiled: 9-10 minutes
  7. Once desired time is reached, turn off heat and carefully transfer all eggs from pot to a clean bowl and move to the sink
  8. Under cold, running water peel each egg, rinse, and transfer to a seprate clean bowl
  9. Once all eggs are peeled, set aside any you plan to save for later in an airtight container
  10. Slice remaining eggs in half (from pole to pole, not along equator) and season to taste with kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper, then enjoy!
